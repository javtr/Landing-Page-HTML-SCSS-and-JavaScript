Learn to Identify Patterns Easily

The high volume area (High Volume Area/HVA) is easily reflected in the chart because it has dark colors and large numbers, where the price takes liquidity from the available orders and makes a small pause when this pattern is generated.

The low volume area (Low Volume Area/LVA) is easily observed because we have lighter colors than the rest of the candlestick, this shows that the price traded faster at these levels because it was not as liquid.

The Low Imbalance is a Low Volume Area that has imbalances of buying or selling (blue numbers). This pattern shows us an initiative in the direction of the imbalances but with little strength, the movement can continue for a few ticks and reverse the movement easily as it is a weak auction.

The High Imbalance is a pattern that occurs when there are buying or selling imbalances (red numbers). Its main characteristic is that it has medium/dark colors, which represents that the auction is intermediate/strong and allows the price to have greater trajectories.
